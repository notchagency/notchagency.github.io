About Us

Archimedes of Syracuse (c. 287 BC – c. 212 BC) was a Greek mathematician, physicist, engineer,
inventor, and astronomer. Archimedes was asked to determine whether some silver had been
substituted by the dishonest goldsmith in the King Hiero II’a crown. While taking a bath, he noticed that
the level of the water in the tub rose as he got in, and realized that this effect could be used to determine
the volume of the crown. Archimedes then took to the streets naked, so excited by his discovery that he
had forgotten to dress, crying "Eureka!" (pronounces as Evrika) meaning "I have found it!”.
